If I were to put my professional life on a timeline, this would be it:

  • UncommonPurpose

    August 2022

  • Tick42

    June 2020

    Everything was going quite well so far, except two things: I started to feel I was doing a "code monkey" job, and I was barely making ends meets (now in a different country/market/cost of living).

    This is when I decided to explore the market in Bulgaria. And lucky me! Someone recommended Tick42 (now interop.io). I had a couple of interviews with them and in less than a week I got a nice offer from them.

    My time at Tick42 was immensely rewarding, surrounded by highly skilled developers and excellent managers. Occasionally I had the opportunity to lead a small team and many times I mentored other developers in my area of expertise, which, by this time, was Backend Development with Java and the Spring ecosystem. I got the opportunity to learn directly from the top management and technical leaders and got my hands on projects where I had to interact directly with our clients.

    As part of my daily job I had to design, develop, test, and maintain back-end features using Java, running on top of Spring Boot. Design and implement business metrics, using Java, Prometheus, and Grafana. And as usual as it is in our profession I had to participate in scrum activities and CI/CD processes, as part of a multicultural agile team.

  • Moved abroad to work for Musala Soft

    April 2019

    By 2018 I was feeling stuck and making little progress in my career path. I decided to look for opportunities abroad, now with 4 years of professional experience under my belt.

    By November of that year I got an offer from Musala Soft , and by the end of March 2019 I had moved to Bulgaria. I started to work officially on April.

    At Musala, I designed, developed and tested product features, using TypeScript/ JavaScript and Java.
    I participate in peer programming with awesome colleagues, from who I learned a lot, daily scrum activities, planning, refinement, retrospective, and knowledge-sharing sessions as part of an agile development team.

  • First time working at an international company

    August 2016

    It wasn't until I started at ISA - Soluciones en Software that I knew how demanding the software industry can be. Throughout my time here, I worked on three products 1 2 3 commercialized mainly in Uruguay and Paraguay.

    My team was located in Havana and worked closely with our colleagues in Montevideo to provide product updates and release. During this time, I designed and develop many of these products' features, using JavaScript, Java, and Groovy.
